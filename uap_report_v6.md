## Citation Standard
Applies to every item in every section. Each item must include a markdown-formatted link to its primary source with visible date provenance — e.g., [NBC News, June 20, 2026](URL). Do not link to aggregators — trace every story to its original publication. If a source is inaccessible, log it in Section 7 rather than skipping it silently.

## Report Structure

Use these fixed section headers on every run, in this order, so reports are comparable over time.

### Date & Sky Event Confirmation
State today's confirmed date and time. List any active astronomical or launch events that could affect sighting credibility. If none, state that explicitly.

### 0. PURSUE Check *(mandatory — run before any other section)*
Check war.gov/ufo for the status of the Presidential Unsealing and Reporting System for UAP Encounters (PURSUE). Report:
- Whether any new tranche has been released since the last report (most recent known: Release 03, June 12, 2026 — 304 total files across 3 releases, 1.7B+ site hits)
- Total file count and agency contributions if updated
- Any official statements about upcoming release timing or scope changes
- Any significant media analysis of existing releases published in the last 48 hours

If the portal is unreachable, log in Section 7.

### 1. Disclosure Pressure *(new — Section 1 on every run)*

This section tracks the current state of the disclosure governance architecture and any developments that accelerate or constrain it. Search specifically for:

**Upcoming disclosure events**
- Any announced press conferences, congressional hearings, public forums, or advocacy events related to UAP disclosure in the next 30 days
- The UAP Disclosure Forum (disclosure.org / disclosureforum.org): scheduled events, speaker announcements, or post-event coverage. Next known event: Washington D.C., June 25, 2026
- Sol Foundation upcoming symposia or working group sessions

**UAP Governance Board & Science Advisory Council**
- Any official government announcements confirming the UAP Governance Board (established mid-June 2026 by ODNI, FBI, Department of War)
- Board membership, charter, Federal Register notices, or formal mandate documents
- UAP Science Advisory Council (announced June 13, 2026 by Avi Loeb): new member additions, meeting announcements, published findings, or government agency confirmations
- Known Science Council members as of last report: Avi Loeb (chair), Garry Nolan, Tim Gallaudet, Michael Shermer, Matthew Szydagis, Carol Cleland, Robin Hanson, and others. Flag any changes.
- Any official government confirmation that one or more named agencies (White House, AARO, ODNI, FBI) has publicly acknowledged the Science Advisory Council's existence

**Trump administration signals**
- Any White House statements, press briefings, executive orders, or presidential remarks (on or off the record) touching on UAP, disclosure, PURSUE, or related programs
- Any reporting on a possible presidential announcement or formal disclosure statement — including sourced rumors with named outlets or reporters
- Signals from senior administration officials (NSA, SecDef, DNI, NSC) that disclosure posture is shifting

**Whistleblower and amnesty developments**
- Any movement on whistleblower immunity or amnesty legislation (related to Rep. Luna, Rep. Burlison, or Sen. Schumer's UAPDA provisions)
- New whistleblower statements or filings — named or anonymous
- Reports of individuals requesting or receiving legal protection before coming forward

**UAP Disclosure Fund**
- New grants, advocacy actions, legal filings, or public statements from the UAP Disclosure Fund (uapdisclosurefund.org)
- Any named individuals or organizations newly supported by the Fund

**International disclosure signals**
- Foreign government announcements, parliamentary debates, or official UAP policy changes
- Statements from allied intelligence services or defense ministries
- Follow-up to Brazil (Rebelo pledge), Australia (Turner/NAA), Israel (Eshed), or other countries previously covered

For each item in this section, note whether it represents: **acceleration** (moves disclosure forward), **delay/obstruction** (slows or blocks it), or **neutral/structural** (new institutional apparatus with unclear effect).

### 2. Sightings
*Date range covered by available database data: [X] to [Y]*

List the most credible recently published sighting reports. Include only reports meeting one or more of these criteria:
- Corroborating photo or video media attached
- Multiple independent witnesses
- Reported by a credentialed observer (pilot, law enforcement, military)
- Anomalous characteristics: transmedium behavior, non-inertial movement, structured craft at close range, or radar/sensor corroboration

If more than 10 reports meet these criteria, include only the strongest 10. For each entry: date/time · location · shape/description · source link per citation standard.

Cross-reference sighting dates against the sky events noted above — flag any probable correlations.

### 3. News & Announcements
Summarize press releases, official statements, or significant public commentary from the last 24–48 hours. Separate U.S. government activity from international and researcher commentary where volume warrants it. Cite per citation standard.

### 4. Legislative & Policy Tracker
Summarize any UAP-related congressional or executive branch activity from the last 7 days: bill introductions, hearing announcements, deadline expirations, document releases, or interagency statements. Cite per citation standard.

### 5. Books & Periodicals
Summarize any recently published or announced nonfiction books or technical and peer-reviewed articles on UAP within the last 30 days. For each item: title · author(s) · publisher or journal · publication or announcement date · brief description of scope or argument · source link per citation standard. Where a book is forthcoming, note the confirmed release date. If no new material has been published in the current window, state that explicitly — do not recycle older titles.

### 6. Explained & Resolved Cases
Briefly note any sightings from the current batch that carry a mundane explanation (Starlink, meteor, Chinese lantern, drone, aircraft, searchlight, etc.). Cite per citation standard.

### 7. Carry-Over Items
On repeat runs, note any items from the prior report that have since been updated, resolved, or require follow-up. If this is the first run, state that explicitly.

### 8. Source Access Log
Log the access status of every source attempted:
- ✅ Fetched successfully
- ⚠️ Partially accessible (note what was and wasn't reachable)
- ❌ App-gated, JS-rendered, or otherwise not fetchable

## Standing Rules
- Every item must be individually traceable to a primary source — cite per citation standard throughout
- Do not fabricate or infer sighting entries — if sources haven't published recently, report what's actually there and state the gap
- Cross-reference sighting dates against sky events noted at the top — flag any probable correlations
- If a source is inaccessible, log it in Section 7 rather than skipping it silently
- After outputting the report in chat, push it immediately to the hub as a UAP Daily note in data.json
- Section 0 (PURSUE Check) and Section 1 (Disclosure Pressure) run on every report — never skip them even if no new material is found; explicitly state the current status

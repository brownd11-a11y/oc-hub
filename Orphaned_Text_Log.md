**[Full original text — "December 16, 2017" subhead section, 3 paragraphs]**

On Sunday, December 16, 2017, the front page of *The New York Times* carried a story headlined "Glowing Auras and 'Black Money': The Pentagon's Mysterious U.F.O. Program."

The program's director had resigned in protest the month before, citing excessive secrecy and internal resistance to further inquiry. On his way out, he gave three Navy videos to journalists. Those videos would change everything.

Alongside the story, the *Times* embedded two declassified Navy videos. One, captured off the coast of San Diego in 2004, shows two F/A-18 pilots tracking a Tic Tac–shaped object that accelerated, rotated, and maneuvered in ways no known aircraft could replicate. The senior pilot, Commander David Fravor, later testified under oath before Congress that "the Tic Tac Object that we engaged in November 2004 was far superior to anything that we had at the time, have today, or are looking to develop in the next 10+ years."

**Paragraph 1 — Classified-tech objection:**
The most common objection is also the most superficially reasonable: that what military pilots and sensor systems have observed is classified American technology, programs so compartmented that even the aviators who encounter them are never told what they saw. The objection has genuine grounding. The United States has demonstrated, repeatedly, that it can conceal an operational aircraft program for years. The F-117A Nighthawk flew combat missions for five years before its existence was publicly acknowledged. The B-2 Spirit and the Tacit Blue demonstrator remained classified for years longer. Crews flew those aircraft under strict orders not to discuss them. Their spouses didn't know what their partners flew. The black budget, the classified portion of federal defense spending, has exceeded fifty billion dollars annually in recent years, funding programs whose codenames do not appear in public documents.

**Paragraph 2 — Objection collapses:**
It collapses under its own logic. Military aviators who had inadvertently encountered a classified American program would have been required to sign nondisclosure agreements on the spot; the pilots in the documented Navy encounters were not. The Defense Department operates dedicated, isolated test ranges (Tonopah, Edwards, China Lake) precisely so that classified platforms are not flown over active carrier groups conducting pre-combat exercises in international waters. When Commander David Fravor was asked by a member of Congress whether the government tests classified weapons next to multimillion-dollar jets without informing the pilots, he answered: "No. We have test ranges for that." In May 2022 testimony, Deputy Director of Naval Intelligence Scott Bray and Undersecretary of Defense Ronald Moultrie both expressed high confidence that the first 144 UAP reports did not include incidents involving secret American aircraft. Whatever these objects are, they predate the capability to build them.

**Paragraph 3 — ICBM interference:**
These craft show concerning behaviors beyond their interaction with military flight operations. Multiple documented incidents record interference with U.S. and Soviet ICBM nuclear launch facilities, including the instantaneous activation and deactivation of missiles across multiple sites. (The Evidence covers these in detail.) Reports of radiation injury have been filed and validated. The phenomena have jammed airborne radar, disabled flight systems, and interfered with flight controls.

**Paragraph 4 — Government duplicity:**
We now know the U.S. Government has internally treated UAP as a serious matter while publicly dismissing the topic and denying their own interest and involvement. Even after the *Times* article, the Pentagon worked to discredit the program's former director and the witnesses who followed him.

**Paragraph 5 — Narrative control:**
Worse, a long trail of evidence shows that the U.S. Government has deliberately controlled the UAP narrative to suppress reporting of events and interest in the topic. This narrative shaping included active disinformation, denial that anything of non-human origin had been observed, and failure to warn of concern about UAP. While the public was told there is nothing of interest in the UAP topic, there were multiple examples of UAP interfering in our military operations. Declassified records show just how concerned the Government really was about these craft. The gap between what was said publicly and what was studied in classified channels is the subject of this book.

**Paragraph 6 — Congressional position:**
This is the position of decorated military officers who testified under oath before Congress, of senior intelligence officials who filed formal whistleblower disclosures, and increasingly of members of Congress on both sides of the aisle who have been denied access to programs they have the legal authority to oversee.
